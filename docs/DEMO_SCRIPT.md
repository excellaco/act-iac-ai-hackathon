# Parcella — Demo Script
**Target time:** ~3 minutes
**Presenter persona:** Val (Valentina Reyes, Housing Policy Analyst, Fairfax County)

---

## Setup (before you start)
- Browser open to Parcella, search bar empty, national map visible
- No panels open

---

## Script

### Opening (15 sec)
> "It's Tuesday morning. I have a departmental meeting on parking reform in two hours, and my director just asked me to come with numbers. Not intuitions — numbers she can put in front of the Board of Supervisors."

> "This is Parcella. Let me show you what I do."

---

### Step 1 — Search (20 sec)
*Type "Fairfax County" in the search bar, select from autocomplete.*

> "I search for my county. I'm not browsing a map — I know where I work."

*Panel slides open on the right. Composite RIS score is visible.*

> "Fairfax County scores a 74 out of 100 on the Regulatory Impact Score. That's a measure of how much our zoning regulations constrain housing development — the higher the score, the more constrained."

*Select the R-MF zone from the zone selector.*

> "I'm focused on R-MF — that's our primary multifamily zone. This is where most new apartment development would actually happen."

---

### Step 2 — Drill into the score (40 sec)
*Expand the Development Cost Impact accordion.*

> "I want to know what's actually driving that number. I expand the Development Cost Impact sub-score."

*Point to parking minimums line item and its cost figure.*

> "There it is. In R-MF, parking minimums are the single largest cost driver — adding an estimated $18,000 per unit to construction costs. That figure comes from HUD Fair Market Rent data and BLS regional cost inputs."

*Point to the confidence badge.*

> "And this badge tells me the extraction confidence is High — that means the AI pulled this directly from the R-MF section of the zoning ordinance, not inferred it. I can say that out loud in a meeting."

---

### Step 3 — Add comparators (30 sec)
*Click "Add county to compare," add Arlington, then Loudoun.*

> "Before I walk into that meeting I want to know where Fairfax stands relative to our neighbors."

*Summary ranking bar is now visible across three jurisdictions.*

> "Arlington scores 58 on its RA6-15 zone — their primary multifamily district. Loudoun scores 67 on PD-H2. Fairfax R-MF is the most restrictive of the three — and Arlington already requires just 0.5 spaces per unit in RA6-15, versus our 2. That's not opinion. That's the data."

---

### Step 4 — What-If simulation (45 sec)
*Click "What-If Policy Simulation." Sliders appear. Move parking minimums from 2 to 1 space/unit.*

> "Now I want to model what happens if Fairfax R-MF moves toward what Arlington already allows. I adjust the parking minimum from 2 spaces per unit to 1."

*RIS score updates. Cost per unit drops. Point to the delta.*

> "The RIS drops from 74 to 61. Estimated cost per unit falls by $18,000. Maximum unit yield on a typical parcel increases."

> "That happened in real time, from a public zoning ordinance and publicly available cost data."

---

### Closing (30 sec)
*Take a screenshot or point to the visible panel.*

> "I'm not exporting a report. I'm taking a screenshot. This panel — the score, the source attribution, the plain-language explanation — goes directly into my briefing deck."

> "When a supervisor asks 'where does that number come from,' I can answer in one sentence. That's what makes this usable."

> "Parcella doesn't tell me what policy to choose. It gives me the analysis to have a defensible conversation about it — before my next meeting, not after a four-year study."
